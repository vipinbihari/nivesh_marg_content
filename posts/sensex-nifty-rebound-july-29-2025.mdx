The Indian stock market breathed a sigh of relief on Tuesday as benchmark indices, the Sensex and Nifty, snapped their three-day losing streak to close with healthy gains. The rebound was driven by a combination of factors, including encouraging quarterly earnings and renewed buying interest in heavyweight stocks.

After a choppy start, the bulls took control in the latter half of the trading session. The 30-share BSE Sensex jumped 446.93 points, or 0.55%, to settle at 81,337.95. Similarly, the 50-share NSE Nifty climbed 140.20 points, or 0.57%, to end the day at 24,821.10.

The broader market also participated in the rally, with the BSE MidCap and SmallCap indices gaining 0.81% and 1.03% respectively. This indicates that the positive sentiment was widespread across the market. The overall market capitalisation of BSE-listed firms rose by approximately ₹2.84 lakh crore in a single session.

![A graph showing the upward trend of the Sensex and Nifty on July 29, 2025.](/images/uploads/sensex-nifty-rebound-july-29-2025/market-rebound.png)

### What Fueled the Rally?

The key driver behind today's market recovery was the impressive Q1 earnings reports from several companies. This provided a much-needed boost to investor confidence, which had been dampened by concerns over a potential India-US trade deal and continuous selling by Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs).

Reliance Industries, a heavyweight on the indices, was one of the top gainers, rising by 2.21%. Other blue-chip stocks that contributed to the rally included Larsen & Toubro, Asian Paints, Tata Motors, and Adani Ports.

Several companies announced their Q1 results that buoyed market sentiment. For instance, Welspun Corp reported a consolidated net profit of ₹350 crore for the June quarter, a significant jump of 41% from the same period last year.

This positive earnings news helped to offset the negative sentiment created by mixed results from some other companies and ongoing uncertainty in global markets.

### The Road Ahead: What to Watch Next

While today's rally has brought some cheer, it is important for investors to remain cautious. Uncertainty over the India-US trade deal continues to be a potential headwind. FIIs have also been net sellers in the Indian market, offloading equities worth ₹6,082.47 crore on Monday, according to exchange data.

Here are a few factors to keep an eye on:

*   **Q1 Earnings Season:** More companies are scheduled to announce their Q1 results this week. These will be closely watched and will play a crucial role in determining the market's direction.
*   **India-US Trade Negotiations:** Any development on this front will significantly impact the market. Investors will be looking for signs of a breakthrough.
*   **FII Activity:** The flow of foreign funds is a key indicator of market sentiment. A reversal in the recent selling trend by FIIs would be a major positive.
*   **Global Cues:** The performance of global markets, particularly in the US, will continue to influence the Indian market. Investors will be watching the upcoming US Federal Reserve policy meeting for cues on interest rates.

For now, the market has found support at lower levels, and the bulls have managed a comeback. However, whether this momentum can be sustained in the coming days remains to be seen.
